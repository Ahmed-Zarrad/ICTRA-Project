Generated by `python scripts/evaluate.py` over the full datasets.

- **Phishing set:** Phishing Pot : 8,608 emails analysed (5 unreadable files skipped)
- **Legitimate control:** SpamAssassin `easy_ham` + `hard_ham` : 2,750 emails
- **Decision rule:** an email is "flagged" when its band is **medium or above**
- **Throughput:** ~11,358 emails analysed per run, dependency-free, single-threaded

## Interpretation

- **Very low false-positive rate (0.44%).** This is the headline design goal.
  The fairness rules (absent auth = 0 points; list/bulk-aware envelope checks;
  brand checks restricted to registered-label combosquatting and known-brand
  anchor impersonation) keep the legitimate control set almost entirely in the
  `low` band. 99.6% of legitimate mail scores `low`.
- **Detection rate of 67% at this threshold, with 99.8% precision.** When the
  analyzer flags an email, it is almost always right. Header authentication and
  sender-identity signals do most of the work (phishing header mean 42.7 vs
  legit 0.2).
- **The residual misses are the genuinely hard cases:** phishing sent from real,
  fully-authenticated free-webmail or compromised ESP accounts, with the lure
  carried in an attachment or image rather than scoreable header/link/text
  signals. These are out of reach for a header/link/language rule engine by
  design.

### Choosing a stricter or looser threshold

The band distribution lets you pick an operating point without re-running:

| Flag at...        | Phishing caught | Legit flagged |
|-------------------|----------------:|--------------:|
| `medium` or above | 67.1%           | 0.44%         |
| `high` or above   | 44.2%           | 0.00%         |

`high+` gives an effectively zero false-positive rate at the cost of recall :
useful if the analyzer feeds an automated action rather than a human reviewer.
